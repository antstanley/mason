# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Atmosphere enthusiasts — people already living on Bluesky and the wider
atproto ecosystem who want a richer discovery surface than their timeline.
They arrive with a handle (their own or a friend's), usually from a shared
`?actor=` link, and browse in relaxed sessions rather than task mode. They
know what a PDS is; they don't need atproto explained, they need it to feel
like one place.

## Product Purpose

mason turns a Bluesky follow graph into a single masonry wall that mixes
posts, standard.site blogs, and video into one endless scroll. It exists
because the atmosphere's best content is scattered across lexicons and apps
that never share a screen. Success is serendipity: a session succeeds when
the user finds something they would never have seen in their timeline — a
blog post from a quiet follow, a trailer a friend linked, a deep cut from
the exploration cohort.

## Positioning

The atmosphere, browsable — the first place the whole open social ecosystem
(Bluesky + standard.site + beyond) feels like one place.

## Brand Personality

Playful, tactile, warm. Bricks you want to touch: chunky radii, a slight
tilt on hover, kiln-fired tones. The wall is alive but never shouting; the
fun lives in the materials (brick, mortar, grout, kiln) rather than in loud
chrome. Voice is lowercase, brick-punning, and brief.

## Anti-references

- **Pinterest**: infinite-mall density, samey cards, engagement-bait
  overlays, save-buttons everywhere.
- **Corporate SaaS templates**: cream backgrounds, gradient CTAs, hero
  metrics, icon-card grids.
- **Doomscroll feeds**: badges, unread counters, urgency mechanics. mason
  is unhurried; leaving is fine, returning is a pleasure.

## Design Principles

1. **Bricks are the stars.** Content carries the color and the interest;
   chrome recedes. Any UI element competing with a brick loses.
2. **Serendipity over relevance.** The design should make room for the
   unexpected — the mixer's exploration picks deserve equal visual dignity,
   never "sponsored-slot" styling.
3. **Materials, not metaphors.** The brick/mortar/kiln language shows up as
   texture, weight, and motion physics — not as literal illustrations or
   mascots.
4. **Unhurried by design.** No mechanics that manufacture urgency. Endless
   scroll should feel like wandering a gallery, not feeding a machine.
5. **A wall is a gift.** Shared `?actor=` links are the growth loop; a wall
   viewed through someone else's handle should feel like a made thing worth
   passing on.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.2 AA: ≥4.5:1 body contrast (≥3:1 large text), full keyboard
navigability including the endless scroll, visible focus states, and
`prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for every animation (brick drop-in,
hover tilt). Videos never autoplay — an accessibility stance as much as a
design one.
